Schengen Visas

Back to FAQs

We recommend starting your visa application process through Star Tours a minimum of six to twelve weeks prior to your planned departure date. This timeframe allows for the necessary document preparation, submission, and any potential processing delays, ensuring that your visa is ready in time for your intended travel. It's always a good practice to apply well in advance to avoid any last-minute complications and to provide ample time for the embassy or consulate to review your application.

To apply for a Schengen visa from the UK, your passport should have a validity of at least three months beyond your intended return date from the tour. This means that your passport should remain valid for the entire duration of your tour and for an additional three months after your scheduled return date. Ensuring that your passport meets this requirement is essential to a smooth visa application process and to ensure you can travel without any issues.

To apply for a Schengen visa, if you are holding a UK Work Visa or a Dependant Visa, it should be issued for a duration of more than six months at the time of your application. Additionally, your UK visas should remain valid for at least one month beyond the completion of your tour. Meeting these visa validity requirements is crucial to the Schengen visa application process, as it demonstrates that you have the necessary authorization to return to the UK after your tour and ensures that your travel plans align with the Schengen visa regulations.

This step is crucial as it involves the collection of your unique biometric data, such as fingerprints and photographs, which is an essential part of the visa application procedure. Rest assured, our support and guidance will help ensure that this process runs smoothly, contributing to a successful visa application.

It is essential to ensure that your travel insurance meets the specified criteria and provides the necessary coverage as per the consulate's guidelines when applying for a Schengen visa.

When applying for a Schengen visa, you should submit your application to the consulate or embassy of the Schengen country where you intend to spend the most number of nights during your trip. This is often referred to as the "country of longest duration." Your choice of consulate should align with your travel itinerary and the specific country where you will have the longest stay. It's important to note that you cannot apply to a different embassy unless your travel plans are adjusted accordingly to align with your chosen consulate for visa application purposes.
